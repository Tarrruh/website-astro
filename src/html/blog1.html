New Telescope Added to Our Observatory

Thanks to the university's generous funding, we've recently added a new 12-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope to our observatory. This acquisition marks a significant upgrade to our observational capabilities and will enhance both our research and public outreach programs.

About the Telescope

The Celestron EdgeHD 12" is a versatile, high-performance Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope that offers exceptional image quality across a wide field of view. With its advanced optical design, it virtually eliminates coma, field curvature, and off-axis astigmatism, making it ideal for both visual astronomy and astrophotography.

Key specifications include:

  • 12-inch (305mm) aperture
  • 3048mm focal length (f/10)
  • EdgeHD optical design for flat, coma-free images
  • StarBright XLT coatings for maximum light transmission
  • Computerized GoTo mount with over 40,000 object database
  • GPS alignment for precise tracking

First Light Experience

Last weekend, club members gathered for the telescope's "first light" ceremony. Despite some cloud cover early in the evening, the skies cleared up around 10 PM, allowing us to test the telescope's capabilities.

Our first target was Jupiter, which was breathtaking through the new optics. The cloud bands were clearly visible, and we could easily spot four of its largest moons. The telescope's tracking system performed flawlessly, keeping the gas giant centered in the eyepiece for extended viewing.

Future Observation Plans

With this new addition to our observatory, we're excited to expand our observational projects. Here's what we plan to observe and study in the coming months:

Planetary Observations

The high-resolution capabilities of the EdgeHD 12" make it perfect for detailed planetary observations. We'll be focusing on Mars as it approaches opposition later this year, as well as continuing our ongoing observation program of Jupiter's atmospheric features.

Deep Sky Objects

The increased aperture will allow us to observe fainter deep sky objects than ever before. We've already compiled a list of challenging galaxies and nebulae that we plan to study, including the Horsehead Nebula in Orion and the distant galaxy cluster in Coma Berenices.

Astrophotography

The telescope's flat field design is ideal for astrophotography. We've acquired a new ZWO ASI294MC Pro camera that pairs perfectly with this telescope, and several club members are already planning imaging sessions to capture detailed photos of galaxies, star clusters, and nebulae.
